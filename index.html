Hotel Plans 2000 dollars at least

BEFORE WE START RAISED FLOORS MEANS TO TAKE OFF YOU SHOES
Here are all the different accommodations
First one: Western Style Hotels
Pros:They are familiar, usually include breakfast, location is generally convenient,
Cons: Can be expensive, rooms will be smaller, its per person, not per room
Second one: capsule hotels
Pros: its cheap, its open 24/7, has a decent bed
Cons: limited storage space, potential to hear people coming and going(extra info there are a bunch of rooms like a hangout room, eating area, massage corner, work corner, and finally it is a spa and capsule hotel. again might vary with different ones)
Third One: Airbnb/Minpaku
Pros: get to experience authentic Japanese living, can be cheap, many places have laundry facilities, you may interact with the host, and have all the amenities for a house, kitchen, etc. Probably do not have to meet other hotel guests, and the location can be anywhere
Cons: Sometimes you need to meet with the host at a specific time, its someones house, theres more pressure so that you wont mess anything up, you are expected to leave things the way you found them, every place has their own rules, location could be no where, no breakfast, and spiders hehe
Fourth one: Minshuku(guesthouse)
Pros: could make good use with all the facilites they offer as we are a small group, usually the cheapest type of accommodations.
Cons: locations are normally not as good, not as quiet and calm
Fifth Place: Ryokan
Pros: Often provide dinner and breakfast could be a factor in where we stay, game rooms/karaoke/swimming pools, all in one facility, located in small towns.
Cons: eating times can be limited, on the pricer side, not good for quick stops.
Sixth Place: Love Hotel :flushed:
Pros: can check in anytime of the day, per room not per person, a lot of amenities, spacious.
Cons: cannot drop off luggage, you just show up not book in advance, location could be odd, and you might get locked in
